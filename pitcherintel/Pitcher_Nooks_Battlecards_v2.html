Nooks Setup Guide

How to set up all 9 cards in Nooks — exact steps, card types, and trigger keywords.

📋 What to Create in Nooks (9 cards total)

  1. 5 Industry Battlecards (General type): Pharma / Life Sciences · MedTech / Medical Devices · Manufacturing / Industrial · Financial Services · CPG & High Tech
  2. 4 Role Archetype Battlecards (Persona type): Enablement Champion · Revenue Leader · Ops & Tech Buyer · Executive Sponsor

🔵 Industry Cards — Setup (General Type)

  1. Nooks → Battlecards → New Battlecard → Type: General
  2. Title: Copy exactly (e.g. Pharma / Life Sciences)
  3. Paste content sections: Open With → Pain Points → Talking Points → Proof → Objections → Customer Story
  4. These are manually opened before each call. Know the company vertical before you dial.
  5. Tip: Keep the Industry card open throughout the call as the foundation. Let the Role card auto-surface on top.

🟣 Role Archetype Cards — Setup (Persona Type)

  1. Nooks → Battlecards → New Battlecard → Type: Persona
  2. Title: e.g. Enablement Champion
  3. Persona Trigger keywords: Copy the keywords from the green trigger box on each card
  4. Paste content: Open With → Pain → Lead With This → Proof → Objections → Seniority Modifier → Story
  5. These auto-surface when Nooks detects matching title keywords from the contact record

🎚 The Seniority Modifier (inside every Role card)

  1. You no longer need separate Seniority battlecards — it's built into the Role card
  2. The Seniority Modifier section at the bottom of each Role card tells you exactly how to adjust your angle
  3. C-Suite → outcomes and ROI · Director → champion + adoption · Manager → day-in-the-life + empathy
  4. This keeps the Seniority context in view at all times without an extra card cluttering the screen

💡 Cold Call Flow (how to use both cards together)

  1. Before the call: Open the relevant Industry card (Pharma / MedTech / etc.)
  2. Call starts: Nooks auto-surfaces Role card based on contact's title
  3. First 10 seconds: Use an "Open With" question from the Role card — these are discovery openers, not pitches
  4. Discovery: Reference Pain Points from the Industry card to validate their specific context
  5. Value: Use Talking Points from the Role card (they care about this functionally) + Industry proof (same vertical proof = credibility)
  6. Objection: Navigate to objections section of whichever card is relevant
  7. Credibility close: Drop the Customer Story — match the industry story to their vertical for maximum relevance
